My first example of this comes from an artist named Drake in the song, The Motto. In the song Drake says, “you only live once, that's the motto, yolo.” At first I loved the song and the meaning behind it. I thought it stood for a positive outlook on life; it really made me think. As I listened to it, I realized that I really do only live once and I shouldn’t hold back in life, but not in negative ways. The song got taken out of context as it became more popular on many different occasions. People began doing dumb and outrageous things. Many teens and college students started saying, “yolo” all the time and it was impacting their decisions to do something rationally. Yolo became a slang word through out teens and young adults when talking about things they did that were “unlike” themselves. Sydney Lupkin of ABC says, “Young adults tweet #YOLO when they don't study, get drunk or drive too fast.” She explains this in her study on the implications of the hot phrase on our nation’s impressionable young ones. There are different maturity levels in everyone; nobody matures on the exact same day or year. Everyone matures at different ages and times; this is the problem. Are people mature by the time their legal? If not, how do you stop it?

Yolo is not the only example by any means. Now-a-days, social media is huge part of our society. It is one of the biggest and most important industries in our world. One of the most popular apps in social media networking is VINE. Since the song, “Bandz A Make Her Dance” came out, twerking (a suggestive sexual dance move) has played a huge part in VINE. I haven’t gone a day without seeing a video on VINE of someone twerking. Lisa Berkman of the Huffington Post talks about society, twerking, and teens, all after Miley Cyrus was non-stop twerking on stage during her live performance at this years Video Music Award’s. Lisa goes on to say, “It’s your teenager’s job to upset you. To push your buttons, society’s limits, and that metaphorical envelope. They have so many ways to do this -- the way they dress, the language they use -- but their tool of choice is most often their music.” Whether people believe it or not, they act out on what they listen to or at least support it. All because of a couple lyrics in a song twerking came alive. People see celebrity icons like Miley Cyrus doing it and they want to imitate them on public social media websites where we can all see it. Many people in our society look up to celebrity music icons as role models, and when they support certain artists they sometimes feel like if they do the same things or imitate the artist it will make them more like the particular artist. It takes a certain maturity level to know that you don’t need to do crazy outrageous things in life just because you see an action or hear a certain lyric by an artist.
