Life in the Lyris...

Each and everyday we are all surrounded by music. Music is played around us constantly in our cars, on the streets, in stores, office buildings, and so many others. People have always enjoyed listening to music while doing daily activities and I think sometimes it’s easy to get caught up in what the artist actually means through the lyrics. Think of that catchy tune that you heard today; think of those lyrics. Do the lyrics represent who you are and or what you stand for? In today’s society, music is huge. Music is a representation of feelings, actions, and even some people decisions. People are driven on a daily basis across the world to do things they wouldn't normally do by music. Song lyrics can trigger people to do things that go against the “norm” of society or their normal attitude towards life. When someone hears certain lyrics in a song it can sometimes give them the attitude that if the lyrics and artist say it’s okay to do, then, “it’s okay” right? I personally believe that a lot of these against “norm” things people are doing caused by song lyrics are based on the maturity level of each individual person. There has to be another way to censor music without only using the parental advisory.

Music is, always has been, and always will be, a huge part of my life. I have an enormous appreciation level for the talent and hard work it takes to create a lot of the music I listen to. Music influences my life in so many ways and I truly believe music is a good thing for society. Music gives so much joy to so many different kinds of people and in many ways brings people together. Although I love good (clean/edited) music, I do still listen to some pretty suggestive music at times. To be honest, my favorite artists are rappers. I love the way rap music pumps you up for a game, gets you ready for a big concert, and gives you that adrenaline rush before going out with your friends. Even though I love rap music, I definitely feel like there needs to be a high maturity level in the person listening to it. The suggestive things these kinds of songs suggest people to do aren’t “acceptable” in society to most, and you need to be mature enough to not feel pressured to do many of them. We have left this decision in the hands of parents but it seems like that really isn’t working. I don't know if it's the lack of parent responsibility or kids going behind their parents back to get suggestive music, but there is definitely a problem being seen. Society does a fair job of censoring music but in no means is it perfect.

This is an issue that I stand in the middle on. On one hand there is a good influence through music and the joy it gives to people. On the other there are bad influences that get people to do and say things that they normally would not do if for the song or a certain lyric.
