Other than storyboards marketers use writing in their demographics which are for those who don't know, statistical data relating to the population and particular groups within it. Demographics are how businesses and marketers find out how to bring their idea/ad to the public and also segment the population. They look something like this:

In this example of a demographic it is talking about the Adult Homeless Health Services Program. The chart is showing the percentage of what race is involved. As you can see the majority of this “pie chart” is Caucasians, and the minority is people of ethnicities that happen to not be white, African American, Asian, or Hispanic.

In an Advertising McGraw Hill textbook, there is an entire chapter dedicated to research and planning. The chapter specializes in how much research benefits businesses and their methodology they get from it. It says, “ Marketing research is the systematic gathering, recording, and analysis of information to help managers make marketing decisions.” And for advertising, “ it's the systematic gathering and analysis of information specifically to facilitate the development or evaluation of advertising strategies, ads and commercials, and media campaigns.” Although they have minor changes they share the same topic in general. Research helps make informed decisions.

In the same McGraw Hill textbook it talks about copywriters who play a huge role in the planning step for marketers. It may be self-explanatory but copywriters are the people that write the copy. The copy is the words that make up the headline and message of an advertisement or commercial. The copy for marketers looks something like this:

In this image the copy is the slogan. This is for Adidas Company and there slogan, “Impossible is nothing.” The copywriter’s job is to write that slogan and import it into the ad. It is not there job to tie it to a certain image; their job is to make a slogan that works well in representing the company.
