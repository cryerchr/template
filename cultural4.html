I listen to a lot of rap music including, Drake, Kanye West, and artists like Justin Timberlake. All of these artists have had instances of trouble with society and their decisions, but to me, that doesn't relate to the music they make. Whatâ€™s said in a track is left on the track. If you like a song by a certain artist, that is it. I don't listen to the criticism and the trouble they get into in their everyday life. I do not feel like everyone in society looks at it that way though.

For example, say someone likes the music Lady Gaga makes or even Miley Cyrus, does that mean they have to act like them or imitate them? No. This is the line of maturity level I am talking about. Just because Miley Cyrus has a popular single does that mean young teen girls need to go get tattoos or a buzz cut? I don't believe so, and I put some of that directly on the impact parents are leaving on their kids. My parents raised me to respect music but to more importantly respect myself. Meaning, yes I do listen to suggestive material but I deal with it in a way that it doesn't mold my life or my decisions.
