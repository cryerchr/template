Course Retrospective Essay

To begin the term we composed essays off of a lived experience that we have all dealt with. I wrote mine on my mission trips to the poverty-stricken city of Guatemala. This was my favorite assignment out of all of them because I was able to write about something I am extremely passionate about. Caring so much about this assignment caused me to do very well on it. I was able to explain the meaning of my trip, the impact it had on my life, and I also got to share it with others. I felt this assignment was so important because what we do in Guatemala should be spread across the world, so that so many more people can do the same as I have. I tried to express my excitement and passion for my trips in my writing by giving examples of what we did there and how they directly correlated with my life today.

At times, I struggled with finding the words that I wanted to say on certain topics but I wouldn't have it any other way. This class is meant to challenge you. I look back to one assignment in particular, the Formal Research assignment. In this assignment I explored my major, and wrote on the literacy importance of that particular field. I am a business major, and literacy is a crucial aspect to being successful along with research. By saying this, I mean that the world and its business are always changing and the way you learn and diversify yourself to it is through research. Being a business major makes it necessary that I know this, so that I can learn how others do things and how to be as successful as possible.
